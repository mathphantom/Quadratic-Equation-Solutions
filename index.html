📚 Understanding the Theory

The number of solutions to a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 can be determined by examining where the parabola intersects the x-axis (horizontal axis). These intersection points are called the roots or zeros of the equation.

✅ Two Real Solutions

Graph crosses x-axis at two points

Example: y = x² - 4

• Discriminant (b² - 4ac) > 0

• Solutions: x = -2, x = 2

• Two distinct real roots

⚠️ One Real Solution

Graph touches x-axis at one point

Example: y = x² - 2x + 1

• Discriminant (b² - 4ac) = 0

• Solution: x = 1 (repeated)

• Vertex touches x-axis

❌ No Real Solutions

Graph doesn't touch x-axis

Example: y = x² + 1

• Discriminant (b² - 4ac) < 0

• Solutions: x = ±i (complex)

• Never intersects x-axis

📝 Solutions Summary Table

This table summarizes the characteristics of quadratic equations based on their discriminant and graph behavior:
